spaCy Labels

1. Named Entity Recognition (NER) Labels
Label Meaning Description
PERSON People Named person, e.g., "John", "Elon Musk".
ORG Organization Companies, agencies, institutions, e.g., "Apple", "NASA".
GPE Geopolitical Entity Countries, cities, states, e.g., "United States", "Paris".
LOC Location Non-political locations, e.g., "Mount Everest".
DATE Dates Absolute or relative dates, e.g., "January 1", "yesterday".
TIME Time Times of day, e.g., "2:30 pm".
MONEY Monetary Values Amounts of money, e.g., "$5 million".
QUANTITY Quantities Measurements, e.g., "10 kilograms".
EVENT Event Named events, e.g., "World Cup", "Olympics".
PRODUCT Product Products, e.g., "iPhone", "MacBook".
NORP Nationalities, Religions, Political Groups E.g., "American", "Buddhist".
FAC Facility Buildings, airports, highways, bridges, e.g., "Statue of Liberty".
LAW Law Named documents like laws, treaties, e.g., "Constitution".
WORK_OF_ART Artistic Works Titles of books, songs, etc., e.g., "Mona Lisa".
LANGUAGE Language Any named language, e.g., "English", "Spanish".
2. Part of Speech (POS) Labels
Label Meaning Description
NOUN Noun Names of people, places, things, e.g., "cat", "house".
VERB Verb Action or state, e.g., "run", "be".
ADJ Adjective Describes nouns, e.g., "happy", "blue".
ADV Adverb Modifies verbs, adjectives, e.g., "quickly", "very".
PRON Pronoun Replaces nouns, e.g., "he", "they".
DET Determiner Words that introduce nouns, e.g., "the", "a".
ADP Adposition (prepositions) E.g., "in", "on", "under".
AUX Auxiliary verb Helping verbs, e.g., "is", "will".
CONJ Conjunction Connects words or phrases, e.g., "and", "or".
INTJ Interjection Exclamations, e.g., "wow", "oops".
NUM Numeral Numbers, e.g., "one", "two".
PART Particle Small function words, e.g., "to" (in "to fly").
PROPN Proper Noun Specific names of people, places, e.g., "John", "London".
PUNCT Punctuation Punctuation marks, e.g., ".", ",", "!".
SYM Symbol Mathematical or currency symbols, e.g., "$", "%".
X Other Unrecognized words.
3. Dependency Labels
Label Meaning Description
nsubj Nominal subject The subject of the sentence, e.g., "John" in "John eats pizza".
dobj Direct object The object being acted upon, e.g., "pizza" in "John eats pizza".
ROOT Root The root of the sentence, usually the main verb, e.g., "eats".
aux Auxiliary Helping verbs, e.g., "is" in "He is eating".
prep Prepositional modifier Prepositions, e.g., "in" in "He lives in a house".
pobj Object of preposition The object of the preposition, e.g., "house" in "He lives in a house".
poss Possessive modifier Shows possession, e.g., "John's" in "John's book".
compound Compound Part of a compound noun, e.g., "ice cream".
advmod Adverbial modifier Modifies verbs or adjectives, e.g., "quickly" in "He ran quickly".
amod Adjectival modifier Modifies nouns, e.g., "big" in "The big house".
attr Attribute Used with linking verbs, e.g., "happy" in "He is happy".
csubj Clausal subject When the subject is a clause, e.g., "What she said" in "What she said is important".
neg Negation modifier Negates a word, e.g., "not" in "He is not happy".
cc Coordinating conjunction Joins words, phrases, or clauses, e.g., "and", "or".
conj Conjunct The second part of a conjunction, e.g., "ice cream" in "I like cake and ice cream".
